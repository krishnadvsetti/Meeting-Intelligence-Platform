Speaker 1:
Thank you. My name is Chip Coppola. I live at 1410 South Pennsylvania Street. I was on the steering committee for the old South Pearl Street area of Platt Park.

I am here to support the bill as amended. The changes, in particular the zoning provisions and lowering the height, were critical to what our neighborhood believes in.

Our neighborhood has multiple restaurants and new developments. We recently completed a two-year process studying parking in our area, and because of the amendment we can support this proposal.

Speaker 2:
Good evening. My name is James Davis. I live at 2380 Ivanhoe Street.

I sold my car when I moved here and I make most of my trips by bicycle. People like me do exist.

As someone raised in a low-income household, I always ask how policy affects working people. One key issue is affordable housing.

Research shows that requiring more parking increases development costs and can make affordable housing harder to build. Reports from national organizations have recommended reducing parking requirements to improve housing affordability.

Speaker 3:
My name is Paul Davidson. I live in Curtis Park.

I was concerned about the parking exemption because it was being applied close to my home. I wondered why large developments could receive exemptions when the policy originally seemed intended for smaller developments.

After studying the issue more closely, I now believe the proposal being considered is a balanced interim solution. However, future planning efforts should continue to examine neighborhood character, preservation of existing buildings, and commercial activity.

Speaker 4:
My name is Sue Glassmaker. I also live in Curtis Park.

I think this bill is a compromise. It slows development enough to prevent unintended consequences while giving the city time to study the issue further.

If only a small percentage of lots are affected, I do not believe the measure will significantly impact development opportunities. I encourage continued discussion and future improvements.

Speaker 5:
My name is Eileen Feldman. I support the amendment as a reasonable compromise.

Denver still needs better transit infrastructure before eliminating parking requirements entirely. Affordable housing, transportation access, and responsible development must all be considered together.

I believe this proposal is a good starting point and can be refined over time.
